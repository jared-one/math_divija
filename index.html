The correct equation is: x = y2 − 4y + 2

Step 1: Understand the Reflection

Let's take any point (x0, y0) on the original parabola, y = x2. Its reflection across the line y = x + 2 will be a new point, (x, y). Our goal is to find a formula that connects x and y.

This reflection has two key properties:

  • The line connecting the original point and its reflection is perpendicular to the line of reflection.
  • The midpoint between the two points lies on the line of reflection.

Step 2: Use the Properties to Create Equations

From the properties above, we can find a general transformation rule. For a reflection across the line y = x + 2, any point (x0, y0) is transformed into a new point (x, y) using these specific formulas:

  • x0 = y − 2
  • y0 = x + 2

These formulas tell us how to find the original point's coordinates if we know the reflected point's coordinates.

These formulas come from solving a system of equations based on the two properties from Step 1:

  1. Perpendicular Slope: The slope of y = x + 2 is 1. A perpendicular line has a slope of -1. So, (y - y0) / (x - x0) = -1, which simplifies to x + y = x0 + y0.
  2. Midpoint on Line: The midpoint is ((x+x0)/2, (y+y0)/2). Plugging this into y = x + 2 gives (y+y0)/2 = (x+x0)/2 + 2, which simplifies to y - x = x0 - y0 + 4.

Solving these two simplified equations for x0 and y0 gives you the transformation rules!

Step 3: Substitute and Solve

We know the original point (x0, y0) must satisfy its parabola's equation:

y0 = (x0)2

Now, substitute the expressions from Step 2 into this equation:

(x + 2) = (y − 2)2

Finally, expand the equation and solve for x to get it into the final format:

x + 2 = y2 − 4y + 4

Subtract 2 from both sides:

x = y2 − 4y + 2
